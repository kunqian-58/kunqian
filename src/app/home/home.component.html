Welcome to Kun's Personal Website

My name is Kun Qian (钱坤 in Chinese). I am currently a researcher at IBM Research. My research is at the intersection of data integration, artificial intelligence, and NLP. The goal of my work is to build intelligent human-in-the-loop machine learning systems for entity understanding and knowledge creation. Before joining IBM Research, I earned my PhD in Computer Science under the supervision of three excellent advisers: Balder ten Cate, Phokion Kolaitis, and Wang-Chiew Tan at University of California Santa Cruz (UCSC). Before coming to the US, I also worked with James Cheng at Nanyang Technological University in Singapore..

    DBLP Profile

    Research Interests: human-in-the-loop machine learning, active learning, deep learning, data integration and data exchange.

    My research interests have been focused on data exchange and information integration to, more recently, human-in-the-loop machine learning. I am interested in building intelligent human-in-the-loop machine learning (particuarly, deep learning) systems, with intuitive UI, for entity-centric AI tasks. Representative systems include LUSTRE (ICDE 2018 demo track), SystemER (VLDB 2019 demo track), and PARTNER (AAAI 2020 demo track). Moreover, one of my latest projects is understanding the explainability for NLP tasks, and we are giving a tutorial at AACL 2020 on this topic.
