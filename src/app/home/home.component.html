Welcome to Kun's Personal Website

My name is Kun Qian (钱坤 in Chinese). I am currently a Research Staff Member at IBM Research. My research is at the intersection of data integration, artificial intelligence, and NLP. The goal of my work at IBM is to build intelligent human-in-the-loop machine learning systems for entity understanding and knowledge curation. Before joining IBM Research, I obtained my PhD in Computer Science under the supervision of Balder ten Cate, Phokion Kolaitis, and Wang-Chiew Tan at University of California Santa Cruz (UCSC). Before coming to the US, I also worked with James Cheng at Nanyang Technological University in Singapore.

Research & Publications

DBLP Page GoogleScholar Page

Research Interests: human-in-the-loop machine learning, active learning, semi-supervised learning, deep learning, explainable AI, data integration and data exchange.

My research interests have been focused on data exchange and integration to, more recently, machine learning with small data. I have designed and developed several intelligent human-in-the-loop machine learning systems for entity-centric AI tasks. Representative systems include LUSTRE (ICDE 2018 demo, COLING 2018), SystemER (VLDB 2019 demo, CIKM 2017), and PARTNER (AAAI 2020 demo, EMNLP 2020).

I am recently interested in semi-supervised learning and active learning techniques that can learn robust deep neural networks from small labeled data.
