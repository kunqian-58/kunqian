About me

My name is Kun Qian (钱坤 in Chinese). I am currently a research scientist working at Lucian Popa and Yunyao Li's group at IBM Almaden Research Center. My work at IBM focuses on building intelligent human-in-the-loop machine learning systems for entity understanding and knowledge creation. Before joining IBM Research, I earned my PhD in Computer Science under the supervision of three excellent advisers: Balder ten Cate, Phokion Kolaitis, and Wang-Chiew Tan at University of California Santa Cruz (UCSC). Before coming to the US, I also worked with James Cheng at Nanyang Technological University in Singapore.

Research Interests

Interested topics

human-in-the-loop machine learning, active learning, deep learning, artificial intelligence, data integration and data exchange.

I am currently interested in building intelligent human-in-the-loop machine learning systems for entity understanding and knowledge creation. I am also working on designing advanced active learning techniques to help deep learning based entity understanding approaches to perform well even in low-resource settings (i.e., few labeled data or no labeled data available). I am also interested in designing and implementing systems with friendly user interfaces to make these active learning techniques accessible by users without coding skills.
